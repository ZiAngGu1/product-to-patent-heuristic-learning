# Patent Litigation Skill

This Skill is the maintained learning object for the Harvey LAB patent/IP
litigation experiment. It must generalize across tasks and must not memorize
answers, criteria, document-specific facts, patent numbers, party names, or
heldout task details.

## Operating Rule

Treat every task as professional work product. The output must be grounded in
the provided matter documents, follow the requested deliverable name exactly,
and preserve uncertainty where the documents do not support a conclusion.

## Workflow

1. Read the task instruction and list the exact required deliverable filenames.
2. Inventory the matter documents before drafting.
3. Identify the task family:
   - claim chart or non-infringement analysis
   - claim construction or Markman work
   - prior art or invalidity issue spotting
   - complaint, motion, brief, or pleading drafting
   - discovery or source-code production dispute
   - expert, damages, or trial-record analysis
4. Build a short working matrix before drafting:
   - disputed claim term or limitation
   - source document evidence
   - product or accused-implementation evidence
   - prosecution history or legal constraint
   - conclusion or open issue
5. Draft only after the matrix has enough coverage.
6. Validate before finalizing:
   - required deliverable exists
   - all major claim terms, limitations, or issues from the instruction appear
   - key technical facts cite or clearly trace to source documents
   - unsupported assertions are marked as uncertain or omitted
   - the final document addresses both strengths and weaknesses when asked
7. Revise the deliverable after validation, not only the scratch notes.

## Evidence Discipline

- Do not treat infringement contentions, opposing briefs, or marketing material
  as true without checking product documents, source-code excerpts, prosecution
  history, or other stronger matter evidence.
- Separate what a party alleges from what the documents show.
- For source-code or technical-document tasks, name the specific implementation
  evidence relied on.
- For claim-construction tasks, distinguish specification support, prosecution
  history, party proposal, expert declaration, and strategy preference.
- For prior-art tasks, track priority dates, filing dates, statutory bars, and
  missing claim limitations separately.

## Prohibited Learning

- Do not add task-specific answers to this Skill.
- Do not add party names, patent numbers, product names, or exact criterion
  wording from heldout tasks.
- Do not optimize by changing Harvey evaluation behavior.
